Abstract

Many AI tasks are emerged in dual forms, e.g., English-to-French translation vs. French-to-English translation, speech recognition vs. speech synthesis, question answering vs. question generation, and image classification vs. image generation. While structural duality is common in AI, most learning algorithms have not exploited it in learning/inference. Dual learning is a new learning framework that leverages the primal-dual structure of AI tasks to obtain effective feedback or regularization signals to enhance the learning/inference process. Dual learning has been studied in different learning settings and applied to different applications.

In this tutorial, we will give an introduction to dual learning, which is composed by four parts. In the first part, we will introduce several algorithms of dual learning from unlabeled data, taking machine translation and image translation as applications. For machine translation, we will cover algorithms including dual semi-supervised learning, dual transfer learning, totally unsupervised machine translation and multi-agent dual learning. For image translation, we will cover algorithms including CycleGAN, StarGAN, cdGAN, MUNIT and more recent techniques/applications. In the second part, we introduce several work on dual learning from labeled data, including (1) data-level dual supervised learning and (2) model-level dual supervised learning. In the third part, we will review several theoretical studies on dual learning and present more applications. At the end of the tutorial, we will describe several challenges of dual learning and discuss future research directions.


Outline

  • Dual unsupervised learning, 25min
